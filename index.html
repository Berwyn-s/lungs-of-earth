Why Forests Matter

Oxgen Producer, Carbon Storage

Forests play a vital role in producing the oxygen we breathe. Through photosynthesis, trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, making forests the planetâ€™s lungs. Additionally, forests store vast amounts of carbon in their trees and soils, helping mitigate climate change by reducing greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Home to 80% Terrestial Biodiversity on Earth

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial species, including plants, animals, and fungi. From towering trees to the tiniest microbes, they provide shelter, food, and breeding grounds. This incredible biodiversity is crucial for maintaining ecological balance and supporting life on Earth.
